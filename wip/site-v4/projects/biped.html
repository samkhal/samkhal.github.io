Bipedal Walking

WPI, Major Qualifying Project: January 2016-Present

This robot, formerly known as "Caminante", was built a few years back but was abandoned without anybody having developed controls for it. As my senior capstone, I'm leading a team of 5 to resurrect this machine, redesign and rebuild much of it's mechanics and electronics, and develop walking controls for it from scratch. The robot has a number of interesting features, foremost among them being the series-elastic driven joints: each joint is connected to a motor through a system of springs and cables. These springs could allow for walking gaits that take advantage of them as energy storage during each stride, thus requiring less energy input from the batteries.

We've already developed proof-of-concept walking controls for the robot in simulation, and are eagerly looking forward to implementing them on the robot. An early version of walking in simulation can be seen here.
