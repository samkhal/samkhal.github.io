I'm Sam Khalandovsky, a Robotics Engineering student at WPI interested in control theory, machine learning, underactuated systems, and all things robotics.

I'm a senior working on my BS and MS degrees. I've done research in a number of areas of robotics and am always working on some project or another. I've worked on the high-level (motion planning, machine learning) and the low-level (controls, embedded programming), as well as in systems integration. I've played with simulation and modelling of a variety of systems. Lately I've been most interested in exploring control of dynamic and under-actuated systems, especially for bipedal walking. I've also been playing with neural networks and reinforcement learning to augment controls of highly dynamic systems.

When I'm not working on projects or research, I enjoy fencing, rock-climbing, and mountaineering. At WPI I'm involved in developing the Robotics Engineering program through the Rho Beta Epsilon Honor Society, where I'm the New Member Officer in charge of interviewing and inducting new members.
